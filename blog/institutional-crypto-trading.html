Institutional Crypto Trading: What It Requires

As institutional capital continues to flow into digital assets, the infrastructure demands of professional traders have fundamentally reshaped what crypto exchanges must offer.

1. Prime Brokerage Services

Institutions require consolidated access to multiple venues, margin financing, and securities lending — features borrowed from traditional finance and now expected in crypto.

2. Custody Solutions

Self-custody is impractical at scale. Institutional players need qualified custodians with insurance, audit trails, and regulatory compliance built in.

3. OTC Desk Access

Large block trades executed on public order books move markets. OTC desks allow institutions to transact at negotiated prices without impacting the market.

4. API & Colocation

Low-latency API access and colocation options near matching engines are non-negotiable for algorithmic trading desks operating at scale.

5. Regulatory Reporting

Institutions face AML, KYC, and tax reporting obligations. Exchanges must support automated compliance reporting to remain viable partners.
