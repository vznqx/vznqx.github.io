Crypto Trading Fees Explained

Trading fees are one of the most overlooked factors when choosing a crypto exchange. Over time, even small percentage differences compound into significant cost advantages or losses.

Maker vs Taker Fees

A maker adds liquidity to the order book by placing a limit order that doesn't execute immediately. A taker removes liquidity by filling an existing order. Most exchanges charge lower fees for makers to incentivize liquidity provision.

Withdrawal Fees

Beyond trading fees, withdrawal fees vary significantly by blockchain network. Choosing an exchange that supports layer-2 withdrawals can reduce costs by up to 90%.

Spread Costs

On exchanges with thin order books, the bid-ask spread itself acts as a hidden fee. Deep liquidity minimizes spread costs, especially for large orders.

Volume-Based Discounts

Most professional-grade exchanges offer tiered fee structures where higher monthly trading volumes unlock progressively lower rates.
