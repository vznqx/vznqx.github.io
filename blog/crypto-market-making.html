What Is Crypto Market Making?

Market makers are the backbone of any liquid trading venue. They continuously post both buy and sell orders, profiting from the spread while ensuring traders can always find a counterparty.

How Market Making Works

A market maker simultaneously quotes a bid price (willing to buy) and an ask price (willing to sell). The difference — the spread — is their compensation for providing liquidity and taking on inventory risk.

Why Exchanges Need Market Makers

Without market makers, exchanges would have wide spreads, poor fill rates, and volatile prices even on small orders. Professional market makers are often contracted by exchanges to maintain minimum liquidity standards.

Algorithmic Market Making

Modern market making is entirely algorithmic. Firms use proprietary models to dynamically adjust quotes based on order flow, volatility, and inventory exposure — often updating prices thousands of times per second.

Risks Involved

  • Inventory risk: holding an asset that moves against you
  • Adverse selection: being traded against by better-informed participants
  • Latency risk: being slow to update quotes in fast-moving markets
